About Me

Developer, Designer, Autodidact

Interactive Media with a Social Conscience

As a Cinema and Media Studies major at Wellesley, most of my coursework has been largely theoretical, and usually with an emphasis on documentary film and new media. Since I began cross-registering at MIT, my interests have become increasingly technical. I've always been interested in programming (even took a Python course at Wellesley in my sophomore year), but it wasn't until my MIT coursework introduced me to the intersections between code and social change that I really opened up to the possibilities. I started seeing problems that code could solve in my daily life: in my Japanese language studies, in my extracurricular activities... everywhere.

So much so that I have chosen to, in addition to my CAMS studies, begin teaching myself Front End Web Development, and now work on a number of side projects to further hone these skills. Now that I've found the purpose in programming, I find it to be an engaging, creative and rewarding experience.

One day, I hope to one day be able to merge my formal education in documentary film studies with new media technologies to create narrative and data-driven, interactive media experiences with the power to change the world for the better.

When I'm not working on my code, I work at the Wellesley College Office of Communications and Public Affairs as a Digital Content Assistant. I can also be found filming sketches for Wellesley College Television (WCTV), producing print and digital media for SeedKit or playing custom maps in Left 4 Dead 2.
